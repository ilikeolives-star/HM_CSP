All about choir!

What is choir?

Choir is a group of singers that perform together, usally performed at schools, churches, organized events, etc.
There is also many different ranges in voice parts when joining choir.
The most common voice parts I see in choir are soprano(highest part for female), soprano 2(lower high part for female),
alto(low part for female),tenor(high part for males), and bass(lower part for males).
There is other voice parts that aren't listed, yet these are the parts you'd commonly see in middle and high school choirs.
Usually with choirs, the group spends weeks or months practicing songs, then there is a big concert or event that these songs are sang at.

Why should you join choir?

Choir can definetly be time consuming, but joining is worth it!
Usually the choir teacher/conductor will help you with training your vocal folds to produce a beautiful sound with your larynx.
The performances can be very nerve racking, but there are more good things about it then bad.
The easiest part about joining is that everyone is on a different level on how trained their voice is.
This can be beneficial, especially if you want help from your peers.
People who do choir also should be able to learn how to read music sheets, which can be helpful if you wanted to learn a instrument.
Also, the songs are usually chosen at a level where the choir can sing it without too much struggle after practicing.
